## A new-generation aortic valve that anchors without sutures

In classic aortic valve replacement the surgeon, after removing the diseased valve, secures the new biological or mechanical valve with 12–15 individual sutures. This step is technically demanding and adds time.

**Sutureless** aortic valves remove that step. The valve sits inside a special stent frame; once positioned, the elastic stent anchors the valve to the aortic annulus automatically. Just three guiding sutures are required.

## Why does it matter?

The shift sounds small, but it shortens operating time meaningfully:

- **Aortic cross-clamp time:** Heart-stopped time is 30–40% shorter than classic AVR.
- **Heart-lung machine time:** Drops by a similar margin.
- **Total operative time:** About 30–45 minutes shorter.

Shorter pump time reduces lung and kidney complications — particularly for older or comorbid patients.

## Who is it for?

Sutureless aortic valves stand out in three scenarios:

- **High surgical risk:** Patients near the limit for open AVR but not ideal for TAVI.
- **Concurrent surgery:** Bypass + valve or multi-valve operations, where shorter pump time matters most.
- **Minimally invasive access:** Right upper mini-thoracotomy, where suturing in a small field is challenging — sutureless makes the operation feasible.

## How is it different from TAVI?

The two are sometimes confused:

| | Sutureless AVR | TAVI |
|---|---|---|
| Approach | Open surgery (mini- or full sternotomy) | Catheter (femoral) |
| Diseased valve | Removed | Crushed, left in place |
| Cross-clamp | Yes (short) | No |
| Estimated valve life | 15–20 years | 10–12 years (long-term data limited) |
| Calcification tolerance | More flexible | Limited in certain patterns |

Think of sutureless as "the surgery closest to TAVI." The two aren’t interchangeable; each has its place.

## Outcomes

Multi-centre studies in experienced hands report:

- 30-day mortality: 1–2% (similar to classic AVR)
- Permanent pacemaker need: 8–10% (lower than TAVI)
- Hospitalisation: 4–6 days
- 5-year valve dysfunction: very low

## Limitations

No procedure is perfect:

- **Slightly higher pacemaker rate** than conventional valves.
- **Anatomic restrictions:** wide aortic annulus or specific calcification patterns may rule it out.
- **Cost:** higher than a conventional bioprosthesis.

## How is it decided?

In consultation we review echocardiography, coronary angiography, and CT angiography (when needed) together. Your surgical risk score, anatomy, and any concurrent surgical needs are weighed jointly.
